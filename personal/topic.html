The Curse of the Ninth: Classical Music Superstition

The Curse of the Ninth is a superstition of the history of classical music. It is a belief that the ninth symphony would be a composer’s last piece, and the composer would die when they worked on the 9th symphony, after finished the 9th or before finished the tenth. It is believed that the curse started from Beethoven. After he finished his last symphony, that was the ninth symphony, he soon passed away and was unable to finish his tenth. Famous composers after Beethoven seemed to affect by this curse. Many composers would not write the ninth symphony in order to avoid the curse. For example, Gustav Mahler chose to not number his ninth symphony to avoid the curse, and thought he beat the curse. However, he died after the complete of the ninth symphony. Of course there were some composers were alive after finishing their ninth symphony, but the coincidence made people believe that those composers who died after the ninth were cursed.
